About

Biography

American tenor, Chase Henry Hopkins was born in St. Louis and completed training with Scott Johnson and Paul Plummer as a member of the Royal Conservatoire of Scotland’s Alexander Gibson Opera School. Previously, he studied at the Dutch National Opera Academy with Rosemary Joshua and Northwestern University with Karen Brunssen.

Previous operatic roles includes Eisenstein Die Fledermaus (Scotland), Don Ottavio Don Giovanni (England), Nemorino L'elisir d'amore, Don Basilio Le Nozze di Figaro (England), Jupiter Semele (England), Oronte Alcina (England), and Telemaco Il Ritorno d'Ulisse in Patria (England).

Professional chorus work includes Aix-en-Provence (France), The Wexford Festival (Ireland), The Grange Park Opera (England), the Zurich Tonhalle (Switzerland), and The Castleton Music Festival (US).

Additionally, recent concert repertoire includes Mozart's Requiem and Mass in C minor, Haydn's Nelson Mass, Monteverdi's Vespers 1610, and Handel's Alexander's Feast.
